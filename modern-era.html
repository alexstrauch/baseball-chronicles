The modern era of baseball

The modern era of baseball is often considered to have started in the post-World War II period. This era has seen significant changes in the game, including racial integration, expansion of the leagues, changes in playing style, and the introduction of new technologies.

Key milestones

  • 1947: Jackie Robinson breaks the color barrier
  • 1969: Major League Baseball expands to 24 teams
  • 1973: The American League adopts the designated hitter rule
  • 1994-95: The players' strike cancels the World Series
  • 2002: The use of Moneyball strategies becomes widespread
